This early-stage decision-support tool helps homeowners, housing managers, condominium representatives, renovation advisors, designers, construction professionals, and public support organizations understand which Deep Energy Renovation pathways may be feasible for a specific building situation.

The tool focuses on practical conditions that shape renovation decisions: budget capacity, access to incentives, coordination in multi-owner buildings, resident disruption, administrative complexity, professional support, timing, supply constraints, and staged renovation.

Its purpose is to support clearer, more informed, and more realistic early-stage renovation decisions.

Why early-stage renovation decisions matter

Buildings are central to Europe’s climate and energy transition. Many renovation projects slow down because priorities, finance, ownership coordination, resident disruption, and first-step decisions are not clear enough at the beginning.

Data connection concept

The web version can combine user inputs with controlled lookup tables and selected external data services. Manual selection remains available where automatic data is unavailable.

1

Location and country context

OpenStreetMap/Nominatim supports city search, coordinates and country detection.

2

Typology matching

TABULA/EPISCOPE-inspired tables can connect country, age band and building type to likely starting conditions.

3

EU building-stock context

EU Building Stock Observatory indicators can provide country-level context cards.

4

Climate and solar context

Eurostat degree-day data and PVGIS-informed assumptions can support climate and solar guidance.
